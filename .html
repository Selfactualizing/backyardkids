How we got here

A year ago, our son went septic after Group Strep B infected and spread through his blood. We took him to the ER when we saw he wasn’t feeling well. After they stabilized him, D was transported to St. Joes Children’s Hospital where further tests were given. 

He had spinal meningitis, multiple strokes, and then non stop seizures. 

Our eyes were open to the need for kids to get care. We learned that many parents would abandon their children once a diagnosis was given because there were too many variables. “How can we afford this?” “What will their quality of life be?,” and so on. We are working on resources to help parents fill those questions with answers so they immediate response isn’t just to bail on the kids who need them the most.

The second aspect of our non profit is, to help the families work through the trauma after. Walking out of the PICU after 3.5 weeks did not bring rainbows and unicorns. It brought a lot of grief, sadness, and unbearable feelings of being lost and not good enough. The thought “how could my body not have protected my child” played over and over in Sarah’s head. 

There’s so much HOPE no matter what the outcome. We are blessed that D is thriving through the developmental stages and doing well so we have more energy to outpour into our community.
